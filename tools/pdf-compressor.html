Free PDF Compressor — Reduce PDF Size for LMS and Email

This PDF compressor reduces file size by re-rendering each page at your chosen image quality level and assembling a new compact PDF. It works best on scanned PDFs and image-heavy documents — the type of assignments and reports that are commonly too large to upload to university portals. Text-only PDFs generated from Word or Excel may see less reduction.

How to Compress a PDF for a University LMS Portal

Most university LMS systems including Moodle, VULMS, FAST LMS, NUST LMS, and COMSATS LMS have upload limits between 2MB and 5MB. If your assignment PDF is larger than the portal allows:

  • Upload your PDF and set the quality slider to 50–65%
  • The estimated output size is shown before you click Compress
  • If the estimate is still too large, reduce quality further
  • Download the compressed file and upload it to your portal

How to Compress a PDF for WhatsApp

WhatsApp allows document attachments up to 100MB, but large PDFs are slow to send and receive on mobile data. For WhatsApp sharing, compress to under 5–10MB using 55–70% quality. The compressed PDF will send and open faster for the recipient, especially on slower connections.

What Types of PDFs Compress the Most?

Scanned documents and PDFs with embedded photos compress the most — typically 50–80% size reduction. PDFs generated from Word, Excel, or PowerPoint (with primarily text and vector graphics) compress less because their content is already stored efficiently. Mixed PDFs with some images and some text fall in between.
