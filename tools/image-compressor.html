Free Online Image Compressor — JPG, PNG and WebP

This image compressor reduces your image file size using the browser's built-in Canvas API and JPEG compression. Drag and drop your image, adjust the quality slider, and download the compressed version instantly. No image is ever uploaded to any server — all compression happens locally on your device.

How to Compress Images for LMS Portals and University Submissions

Most university LMS systems (Moodle, VULMS, Google Classroom, FAST LMS, NUST LMS, COMSATS LMS) have strict file size limits for image uploads — typically between 200KB and 2MB. If your image is too large to upload, use this tool:

  • Upload your image and set quality to 65–75% for LMS uploads
  • Check the compressed size in the stats panel — reduce quality further if still too large
  • Most photos will compress to under 300KB at 70% quality with no visible quality loss
  • Download and upload the compressed version to your portal

Compress Images for WhatsApp, Email and Social Media

Large image files slow down sharing on WhatsApp and fill up email attachment limits. For WhatsApp and messaging apps, compress to under 1MB using 70–80% quality. For email attachments that need to look professional, use 80–85% quality. For social media thumbnails where speed matters more than perfect quality, 65–75% is optimal.

How to Compress Images for a Website

Page speed is a Google ranking factor. Large uncompressed images are the number one cause of slow page load times. For web images, target under 200KB for above-the-fold images and under 500KB for all other images. A quality setting of 75–85% typically reduces a 3MB photo to under 300KB with no perceptible quality difference to the human eye.
