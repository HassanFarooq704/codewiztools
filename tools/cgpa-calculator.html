How Does Our CGPA & GPA Calculator Work?

Measuring your academic performance indices can be stressful during fast-paced university results timelines. Standard GPA & CGPA Calculation Methodology

The application handles your distinct grade parameters based on the universally accepted global algorithm: GPA = Total Quality Points / Total Credit Hours. Each singular letter grade translates into an exact predefined mathematical value (such as An A/A+ standard grade equals 4.00, a B equals 3.00, and a C equals 2.00). Multiplying the course credit hour value by the weight of the earned grade scores provides your true Quality Points per class structure.

Difference Between SGPA and Cumulative CGPA

SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average) evaluates your operational grade scores inside a single term workload module. Conversely, CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) computes your entire unified academic progression parameters across all finished semesters. This tool seamlessly switches between both evaluation metrics to support custom grading schemas optimized for local standards, such as the LGU CGPA criteria and general HEC university patterns.
